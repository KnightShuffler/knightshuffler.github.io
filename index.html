About Me

I am a computer science graduate with an interest in theoretical computer science, physics and game development.

I started programming in C++ when I was in high school, motivated to make my own video games. At the end of 12th grade, I made my first game prototype entirely from scratch: Swine Flew. It was a simple side-scrolling game where the player controlled a flying pig trying to reach the end of the level while avoiding obstacles.

Going to university, I was able to explore my interests further. Through writing shaders for games, I learned how to use program GPUs to train neural networks. And, my appreciation for physics led me to start studying quantum computing.

I usually work in C++ and I'm also familiar with Python, C and Java.
